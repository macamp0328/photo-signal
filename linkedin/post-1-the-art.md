## Draft

Last year I opened an art exhibit.

It's in my bathroom.

I shot ~100 photographs at SXSW concerts over the years. Live music I cared about. Strangers in dark rooms, blurred stage lights, moments I wanted to hold onto.

So I printed them. Framed them. Hung them on the wall.

Then I built something: point your phone at any print, and it plays the song from that concert. Not a QR code. Not a tag. The camera just recognizes the photo. Recognition fires in about a second. The song starts.

I wanted the gallery to feel alive. Like the wall remembers.

The whole thing is deployed as a web app. The repo is public. And I didn't write a single line of code to build it.

Next post: how that worked — and what it actually means to engineer something when AI is doing the implementation.
